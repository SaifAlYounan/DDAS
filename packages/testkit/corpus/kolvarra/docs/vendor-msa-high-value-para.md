# Internal Approval Memo — Extension of the Halcyon Machining MSA

To: Group Procurement Committee
From: S. de Ruiter, Category Manager Drivetrain
Date: 6 April 2026
Subject: Extending master services agreement KV-MSA-2021-003 with Halcyon Drivetrain Components Inc.

Colleagues,

We propose to extend our existing master services agreement with Halcyon Drivetrain Components Inc. rather than retender. Halcyon has machined and heat-treated our drivetrain housings since 2021 with zero critical quality escapes, and this extension is a renewal of the Master Services Agreement signed in 2021 on an unchanged commercial architecture.

Commercially, the extension binds Kolvarra to committed spend of USD 2,000,000 across the whole extension period, billed monthly as work orders are released, on net-60 terms. Pricing stays denominated in US dollars throughout.

The extension runs for twenty-four (24) months starting 1 July 2026. We keep our exit flexibility: either side can walk away for convenience with ninety (90) days' notice, paying only for work already performed.

On risk: the liability regime of the 2021 contract carries over unchanged, meaning each party's exposure is capped at twelve months of trailing fees, with the usual carve-outs. Halcyon remains investment grade — Fitch rates the company A- on its long-term issuer default scale, last affirmed in February.

Two housekeeping points for the committee's file. First, the work is plain commercial machining; it is not a regulated activity and needs no licence or authority approval anywhere in the chain. Second, logistics are unchanged: parts continue to move cross-border from Halcyon's plant in Wilmington, Delaware, United States, into our Eindhoven line.

There will be no announcement, no press activity, and nothing external whatsoever connected to this extension. Inside the company it touches the Procurement, Engineering, and Quality functions, which have all reviewed the draft.

Recommendation: approve the extension as described.
